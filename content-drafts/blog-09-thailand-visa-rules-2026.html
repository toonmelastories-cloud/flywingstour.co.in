The Timeline: How We Got Here

  • Before May 2026: Indian passport holders enjoyed visa-free entry to Thailand for stays of up to 60 days — one of the most generous arrangements Thailand offered any nationality, and a major reason Thailand became a top short-haul destination for Indian travellers.
  • 19 May 2026 — the announcement that caused panic: Thailand's Cabinet approved a restructuring of its immigration framework that would have removed India from the visa-free list entirely, replacing it with a 15-day visa-on-arrival requiring a fee of roughly 2,000 THB (about ₹5,000) paid at the airport.
  • Late May – June 2026 — the fallout: The proposal triggered real confusion among Indian travellers — many assumed Thailand had become visa-required overnight — and bookings from one of Thailand's most important source markets measurably declined in the following weeks.
  • 14 July 2026 — the reversal: Facing the drop in Indian arrivals, Thailand's Cabinet reversed the May decision. Instead of reverting to the old 60-day scheme, it went further: India was added to Thailand's 30-day visa-free list on a permanent basis — a lasting entry, not a temporary extension.

Why Did Thailand Almost Scrap Visa-Free Entry?

The May proposal was framed around tightening Thailand's broader immigration and tourism-visa framework — addressing concerns about long-stay visitors overstaying tourist terms and, in some cases, working informally while on a tourist entry. India was one of several nationalities caught in a wider policy tightening, not singled out specifically. The reversal in July reflects something Thailand's tourism economy takes very seriously: Indian travellers are one of its largest and fastest-growing inbound markets, and the dip in bookings after the May announcement made the economic cost of the stricter rule clear within weeks.

Documents to Carry Regardless of Visa-Free Status

"Visa-free" is not "document-free" — Thai immigration, like most countries, still checks the following on every visa-free entry:

  • Passport valid for at least 6 months from your travel date
  • A confirmed return or onward flight ticket
  • Proof of accommodation for your stay (hotel booking or address)
  • Evidence of sufficient funds for the trip, if asked

These are exactly the documents we prepare as a checklist for every Thailand booking — visa-free entry removes the application, not the paperwork discipline.

Where to Go in Thailand: A Quick Route Map

With entry sorted, the actual trip-planning question is which of Thailand's very different regions to prioritise:

  • Bangkok: temples (Wat Arun, Wat Pho, the Grand Palace), floating markets, rooftop bars and some of Asia's best street food — 2–3 days is the usual anchor for any Thailand itinerary.
  • Phuket: Thailand's biggest beach resort island, with nightlife in Patong and quieter bays further south — the easiest beach add-on for a first Thailand trip.
  • Phi Phi Islands: a day-trip staple from Phuket or Krabi — limestone cliffs, Maya Bay, and some of the clearest water in the Andaman Sea.
  • Krabi: a calmer alternative to Phuket, with Railay Beach's dramatic cliffs and easier access to island-hopping.
  • Chiang Mai: the north's cultural capital — temples, night markets, and cooler hill weather, best paired with Bangkok on a longer trip.

Our Thailand tour packages combine two or three of these into a single itinerary, timed to make the most of your visa-free 30 days without feeling rushed.

Do Indians need a visa for Thailand in 2026?

Why did people think Thailand ended visa-free entry for Indians?

Because it briefly, officially planned to. Thailand's Cabinet approved a shift to a 15-day paid visa-on-arrival on 19 May 2026, which made headlines and caused real confusion. That decision was reversed on 14 July 2026, before it was implemented, in favour of a permanent 30-day visa-free arrangement — better than what almost happened, though shorter than the original 60-day scheme.
