Our Proven 5-Day Kashmir Itinerary from Chandigarh

This is the day-wise plan from our best-selling Kashmir Family 4N/5D package, refined over hundreds of departures:

  1. Day 1 — Arrival & Houseboat Evening: Land in Srinagar (or arrive by road), check in to a traditional carved houseboat on Dal Lake. Sunset shikara ride past floating gardens and the old lake neighbourhoods, kahwa on the deck, dinner on board — the perfect first evening.
  2. Day 2 — Srinagar & the Mughal Gardens: Morning at Nishat and Shalimar gardens with the Zabarwan hills behind them, Chashme Shahi's spring water, and Hazratbal's lakefront views. Afternoon in the old city for pashmina, papier-mâché and walnut-wood shopping (your driver knows the fair-price stores). Move to a Srinagar hotel or keep the houseboat.
  3. Day 3 — Gulmarg: Drive about 1.5–2 hours to the "Meadow of Flowers". Ride the famous Gulmarg Gondola — Phase 1 to Kongdoori, and weather permitting Phase 2 towards Apharwat at nearly 4,000 m. Summer brings flower meadows and pony trails; winter brings snow play and ski lessons. Evening back in Srinagar or overnight in Gulmarg.
  4. Day 4 — Pahalgam: Drive to the Lidder Valley (about 2.5–3 hours) past the saffron fields of Pampore. In Pahalgam, take the pony or local cab circuit to Betaab Valley and Aru Valley — pine forests, river bends and meadows that have starred in half of Bollywood. Overnight in Pahalgam or return to Srinagar.
  5. Day 5 — Departure (or extend): Fly out of Srinagar, or add a night for Sonmarg's glacier valley (summer) or offbeat Doodhpathri's silent meadows before heading home to Chandigarh.

9 Practical Tips from Our Kashmir Desk

  1. Fly one way minimum. Keep the road for the scenery, but do not bet both legs of the trip on one highway's weather.
  2. Insist on the houseboat night. Guests who "save" by skipping it regret it every single time — it is Kashmir's signature.
  3. Book the gondola in your package. Phase 2 tickets are limited and sell out in season; on-the-spot queues waste half a Gulmarg day.
  4. Carry cash for small purchases. Cards and UPI work in Srinagar city, but connectivity in Gulmarg/Pahalgam markets can be patchy.
  5. Layer, whatever the month. Even August evenings on Dal Lake need a light jacket; winter needs proper thermals, not just a heavy coat.
  6. Buy saffron and dry fruits at Pampore or government-verified stores — your driver will steer you past the tourist-trap shops.
  7. Keep buffer time on travel days. Mountain weather delays flights and closes passes; never plan a same-day onward connection that cannot flex.
  8. Travel with any photo ID for every member — hotels and houseboats register all guests, including children.
  9. Book peak windows 60+ days out. Tulip season and Christmas–New Year houseboats and Gulmarg hotels genuinely sell out.

Is there a direct flight from Chandigarh to Srinagar?

Non-stop Chandigarh–Srinagar flights operate on a seasonal schedule, and one-stop connections via Delhi run year-round with multiple daily options. Our ticketing desk checks live schedules for your dates and books the fastest routing at the lowest current fare.

What is the road distance from Chandigarh to Kashmir?

Roughly 600 km to Srinagar via Jammu on NH44, crossing the Banihal tunnel into the Valley. Most travellers split the drive with an overnight halt at Jammu, Katra or Patnitop and reach Srinagar fresh the next afternoon.

How many days are enough for Kashmir?

4 nights / 5 days covers the classic trio — Srinagar with a houseboat night, Gulmarg and Pahalgam. Add a fifth night for Sonmarg (summer) or a slower two-night Pahalgam stay. Less than 4 nights means cutting one of the three, which we do not recommend on a first trip.

When does it snow in Gulmarg?

Reliable snow typically arrives by late December and holds through February, which is also when Gulmarg's ski season peaks. For guaranteed deep-winter snow with functioning gondola access, January is the safest bet — and book flights, since the highway is least reliable then.

Do I need a permit or passport for Kashmir?

No. Jammu & Kashmir is a domestic destination — any government photo ID (Aadhaar, driving licence, voter card) is all you need for flights, hotels and houseboats, for every member of the family.
